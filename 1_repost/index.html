Sixty's Fashion

Women's 1960s fashion was extreme style and attitude from the start of the decade to the end.
In the early years, the fashion idol was Jackie Kennedy with her perfectly white pearls and tailored suit dresses. By the the middle of the decade, supper model Twiggy had women freeing their minds and bodies into clothing that didn't require any extra thought or effort.
From modest to "there is no such thing as too short," 1960s fashion was in many ways like the 1920s flapper revolution. But it didn't happen overnight.
